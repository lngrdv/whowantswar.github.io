Who Wants War?

Data Visualization of the International Arms Transfers from 2013 to 2017

If they don’t buy it from us, they’re going to buy it from Russia or they’re going to buy it from China, or they’re going to buy it from other countries. Donald Trump

Despite of many public discussions concerning the global arms control and the need to limit the spread of military technologies around the world, the trend in international transfers of major weapons continues to be upward. This data visualization helps you to explore this tendency. It contains the export and import data of 25 largest importers and exporters of major arms in 2013–2017, including their main clients, suppliers, and types of weapons. The data is provided by the SIPRI Arms Transfers Database.

What should I know about the
SIPRI data collection method?

COVERAGE

This data relates to actual deliveries of major conventional weapons. The SIPRI Arms Transfers Database covers all international sales, gifts of weapons, and manufacturing licences.

FIGURES

Figures are SIPRI Trend Indicator Values (TIVs) expressed in millions. The SIPRI trend-indicator value is a measure of the volume of international transfers of major weapons. More detailed description of this method can be found at the SIPRI website:
https://www.sipri.org/databases/armstransfers/sources-and-methods.

TYPES OF WEAPONS

SIPRI covers only major weapons. You can check out the specific definitions for each type of weapon here:
https://www.sipri.org/databases/armstransfers/sources-and-methods.

Exports/Imports by Regions

The chart indicates that countries outside Europe and America play only a small role in the global export of arms.

The top five exporters in 2013–2017 – the USA, Russia, France, Germany and China – exported 11 per cent more arms than the countries comprising the top five in 2008–2012.

The main recipient regions in 2013–2017 were Asia and Oceania and the Middle East.

The flow of arms to the Middle East and Asia and Oceania increased between 2008–2012 and 2013–2017, while there was a decrease in the flow to the Americas, Africa and Europe.
